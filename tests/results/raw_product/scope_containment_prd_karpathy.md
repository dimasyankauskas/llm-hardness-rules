## Problem Statement
Users currently spend too much time navigating the full restaurant directory to find and reorder from their preferred vendors. This friction leads to decision fatigue, increased time-to-order, and potential cart abandonment when a user is "hangry" and simply wants their usual meal.

## Target Persona
**The "Routine Diner" (Alex):** A busy professional who orders lunch or dinner 3-4 times a week. Alex has 5-6 "go-to" restaurants and values speed and efficiency over discovery. Alex wants to complete an order in under 60 seconds.

## Core User Flow
1. **Discover:** User views a restaurant profile or their order history.
2. **Tag:** User taps the "Star" icon on a restaurant to add it to their "Favorites" list.
3. **Access:** User navigates to the "Favorites" tab from the home screen or bottom navigation.
4. **Select:** User taps a favorite restaurant to view their most recent or "usual" order from that vendor.
5. **Convert:** User taps "Quick Reorder" to add the items to the cart and proceed directly to checkout.

## Success Metrics
1. **Reduced Time-to-Order:** A 25% decrease in the average time elapsed from app launch to order confirmation for repeat customers.
2. **Feature Adoption:** 40% of monthly active users (MAUs) have at least three restaurants saved to their favorites within the first 60 days.
3. **Order Frequency:** A 15% increase in month-over-month order frequency among users who have favorited at least one restaurant.
